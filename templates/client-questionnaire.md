# Client Website Questionnaire

Use this with prospective clients before starting a website project. The answers feed the internal project brief.

Ask clients to answer in plain language. Short answers are fine, but specific examples are better than polished marketing copy.

## 1. Business Basics

1. What is your business or brand name?
2. What website domain should this project use?
3. Who is the main contact for the website project?
4. What does your business do? Explain it like you would to someone you just met.
5. Where do you serve customers? Local, regional, national, or international?

## 2. Offers

1. What services, products, or content do you offer?
2. Which offer matters most for this website?
3. Are there any offers you do not want to promote right now?

## 3. Audience

1. Who is your ideal customer or reader?
2. What problem are they usually trying to solve?
3. What do they say when they describe that problem?
4. What questions do they ask before choosing you?
5. Are there any audiences you do not want the site to focus on?

## 4. Goals

1. What is the main thing you want visitors to do?
2. What are one or two secondary actions visitors should be able to take?
3. Six months after launch, what would make this website feel successful?

## 5. Trust And Proof

1. Why do customers, readers, or clients choose you?
2. What makes you different from competitors or similar creators?
3. What credentials, certifications, awards, reviews, testimonials, case studies, or numbers can we use?
4. What might make someone hesitate before contacting or following you?

## 6. Brand Feel

1. Choose 3-5 words that describe how the site should feel.
2. Should the writing be formal, business casual, conversational, technical, or something else?
3. Are there colors, styles, websites, or brands you like?
4. Are there colors, styles, websites, or brands you dislike?

## 7. Content And Assets

1. Do you have a logo?
2. Do you have photos, videos, graphics, or brand files?
3. Do you have existing website copy, brochures, social posts, or articles we should reuse?
4. Do you need help writing new copy?

## 8. Pages

Which pages do you need at launch?

- Home
- About
- Services or Offers
- Individual service pages
- Portfolio or case studies
- Blog or articles
- Contact
- Privacy Policy
- Terms of Service
- Other

For each page, describe what a visitor should learn or do there.

## 9. Technical Needs

1. Who will update the website after launch?
2. Do you need a CMS/admin panel?
3. Where should contact form messages go?
4. Do you need analytics?
5. Do you need booking, payments, CRM, newsletter, chat, maps, or other integrations?
6. Are there legal or accessibility requirements we should know about?

## 10. Scope Boundaries

1. What should not be built at launch?
2. What can wait until a later phase?
3. Are there deadlines or launch events we need to hit?

## 11. Inspiration

List 2-4 websites you like. They do not need to be in your industry.

For each website:
- URL
- What you like about it
- What you do not want copied
